It started with Fansites

So now you may be thinking how on earth did I manage that and it boils down to fansites. My first ever fansite was on twilight.

But before the fansite, it started with a community on twitter that lead to me learning...

But before the fansites it started with me being involved with a huge and I when I say huge it was like thousands of people in this twilight fandom on twitter.

They introduce me to fansites and to blogging. Most importantly they got me sucked up into a whole new world I didn't even know existed.

I met people who taught me about how to blog, about blogging platforms such as wordpress. When I first started I didnt know the first thing to bringing a site to life.

So when my first fansite was made, it was made by someone who made a business out of designing and building wordpress themes for fansites. And there are many women out there doing that as their main job or their side job and I thought that that was amazing and I wanted to do something like that for a living.

So when I finally got my first fansite up and running I needed to learn how to maintain it, so my nights and weekends after work became consumed with how to get my site to where I wanted it to be. And these amazing women introduced me to programs like photoshop and illustrator so I can learn how to make the graphics that I wanted to add to the site. Then they introduced me to programming languages like html and css and php. So that gave me even more things I needed to learn in order to do what I wanted to do on the site.

During the day at work when I had free time, it became my way to keep reading up on everything I was trying to learn how to do with my new site. I became obsessed and I was excited about everything I was learning and doing. I really wanted to get better at it so that one day I could possibly be like those women who made a living designing fansites.

So when I got laid off from my job, it became a blessing in disguise because one of those ladies I met in the twilight fandom convinced me that getting laid off was the best thing that could ever happen to me.

I took that as my sign to go back to school to learn how to become a web designer and actually get a job in the field. And that is exactly what I ended up doing.

I didnt know about the bootcamps at the time so I went back to school for another bachelors degree in web design. While in school it really helped me realize my passion was for coding and not really the design aspect.
